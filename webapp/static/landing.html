Why QR Codes Are Being Weaponized

Quishing exploits the trust gap in QR code scanning — your phone reads whatever URL is encoded, with no way to verify legitimacy before the redirect happens.

Phishing Redirect

Malicious QR codes redirect users to spoofed login pages that harvest credentials. Standard URL filters cannot inspect QR payloads in real-time.

CVE-2024-QUISH

MITM Interception

Attackers intercept QR authentication challenges mid-session, substituting their own QR or relay the victim's scan to authenticate as them.

MAN-IN-MIDDLE

QR Overlay Attacks

Physical sticker overlays on legitimate QR codes in public spaces redirect users to credential-harvesting sites disguised as payment portals.

Four-Step Hardware Defense

Each authentication attempt passes through cryptographic splitting, IoT confirmation, and physical device approval — making phishing mathematically impossible.

01

Visual Cryptography Split

The authentication QR is split into two cryptographic shares using (2,2) Visual Cryptography. Neither share alone reveals any information.

VC-Engine · Python · PIL
02

QR Delivered to OLED

The generated QR payload is published over MQTT and rendered on the ESP32 OLED so the customer can scan only from trusted hardware, not a spoofed screen.

MQTT Payload · ESP32 OLED · Trusted Display
03

Hardware OTP via MQTT

A time-bound 4-digit code is generated and sent over MQTT to your registered ESP32 device. It appears on the OLED display — nowhere else.

MQTT · HiveMQ · ESP32
04

Cryptographic Approval

User enters the hardware-displayed code to prove physical possession. Mismatches are rejected instantly — phishing relays cannot replicate this.

Flask · Session · HMAC
